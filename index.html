The Legend

Three Figures. One Root. A Story Older Than Kentucky.

They say the root doesn't show itself to just anyone. It takes a certain kind of soul — one who knows how to listen to the land. The old ones called it a gift. The hunters called it a calling. And somewhere in the hills of Appalachia, the legend of Ancient Proof begins with three figures who have walked these hollers since before memory.

⛏️ Hez — The Sang Hunter

He's been digging longer than anyone can remember. Walks with a mattock like an extension of his arm. Knows every hollow, every slope, every place where the root sleeps. They say he found the oldest ginseng in America — a root that predates the country itself.

🦊 Vesper

The fox appears where the root grows strongest. Old Appalachian hunters took her as a sign — follow the fox, find the sang. She's the land's own messenger, leading the worthy deeper into the hills. She doesn't speak. She doesn't need to.

👑 The Ginseng King

In Korean and Chinese legend, the spirit of the oldest root walks as a king — ancient, wise, and impossible to fool. When a root grows for two hundred and fifty years, they say the King himself guards it. Ancient Proof carries his crown in every bottle.
