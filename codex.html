The Codex of the Root

One Root.
Three Lands.
One Bloodline.

For a thousand years the root was worshipped on three continents as a living spirit. Ancient Proof is the whiskey that finally caught it in a bottle. Open the book.

Three Lands, One Bloodline

The same root, revered across three cultures — the line begins in the East and comes to rest in the hills.

Korea

Insam — the human root

Son of the Mountain God Sansin. The most prized roots flee, glow, and take the shape of a laughing child. Where the bloodline begins.
China

Renshen — the man root

Personified as the Ginseng King — the divine ginseng of the old chronicles, a crowned spirit of gold walking out of legend.
Appalachia

Sang — the mountain root

Diggers follow an unwritten Code so the line endures. Where the spirit came to America and found new ground.
The Cast

Seven figures carry the root from the old world into the glass.

The Ginseng King

The Korean origin spirit. A root grown past two centuries until it stood up and walked as a crowned man of gold. From insam to renshen, every chronicle of the East names him. He is the first father of the line.

“Every root in this house descends from him.”
